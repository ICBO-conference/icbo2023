Customs and Immigration

Brazil adopts a policy of reciprocity regarding visas. This means that nationals of countries that require visas for Brazilian citizens will need a visa to travel to Brazil. According to the Brazilian law, Brazil must agree, on a reciprocal basis, to offer visa waiver to nationals of another country, except for the hypotheses established in the Decree nº 9.731/2019. Brazil has signed visa exemption agreements with about 90 countries.

To find out if you need a visa to travel to Brazil, click here.

Where to apply for a visa? Itamaraty (the Ministry of Foreign Affairs) is the body of the Brazilian government responsible for granting visas, which occurs through the Embassies, General Consulates, Consulates and Vice consulates of Brazil abroad. Here you can visit the Ministry of Foreign Affairs visa application here.

Travel to Brasilia

Brasilia, the federal capital city of Brazil, is located in the central-west region of Brazil on the central plateau of Brazil.

Airport

The Presidente Juscelino Kubitschek International Airport (IATA: BSB) is the only international airport serving Brasília. Brasília Airport has direct flights to all Brazilian capitals and to 19 other Brazilian cities. There are 45 Brazilian destinations connecting Brazil with the capital of the Republic. Brasília Airport also has direct flights to/from six direct international destinations: Buenos Aires in Argentina, Cancun in Mexico, Miami and Orlando in the United States, Lisbon in Portugal, and Panama City in Panama.
Most flights to Brazil go to either Rio de Janeiro or São Paulo, where you can get a connection to Brasilia.

  • Airlines: LATAM, AZUL and GOL are Brazilian carrier serving many countries. But you also could fly from other such American Airlines, Delta and United, Air Canada, TAP Air Portugal, Aerolíneas Argentinas, Copa Airlines and others.

Local Attractions

Brasilia was the first modern and youngest city to be declared a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 1987. The city's initial project was conceived by urban planner Lúcio Costa and the main buildings were designed by architect Oscar Niemeyer. Thus, there are many monuments and other attractions to be visited in the capital.

Take a look in the official City Tour Guide of Brasilia and in our tourism sightseeing recommendations from the official website of the Government of the Federal District.
